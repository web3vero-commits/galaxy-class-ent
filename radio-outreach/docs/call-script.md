# Radio Promoter Cold Call Script
## Galaxy Class Entertainment — Michael Zdanowicz

---

### Before You Dial

- Confirm you have the right direct line (MD or PD, not receptionist)
- Know the station format and a current artist that fits
- Have the artist one-sheet ready (name, genre, single title, streaming link)
- Best windows: **Tuesday–Thursday, 10:00–11:30 AM local time**

---

### Opening (Receptionist / Gatekeeper)

> "Hi, this is Michael Zdanowicz calling from Galaxy Class Entertainment in Orlando.
> I'm a regional promoter — I'm trying to reach whoever handles music adds
> for [FORMAT] on [CALL LETTERS]. Is that [MD NAME] or someone else?"

**If transferred:** go to Music Director opening below.
**If asked what it's regarding:**
> "I have a new [COUNTRY / POP / R&B] single from one of our artists — I just want to
> get it in front of the right person. Takes 30 seconds."

---

### Music Director Opening

> "Hey [NAME], this is Michael Z — I'm the founder of Galaxy Class Entertainment,
> we're a management and promotions firm out of Orlando and Nashville.
> I've got a [FORMAT] track I think is a fit for your station —
> do you have 30 seconds or should I call back at a better time?"

**If yes:**
> "The artist is [ARTIST NAME] — [ONE LINE BIO: genre, style, recent milestone].
> The single is '[TITLE]' — it's doing well in [MARKET / STREAMING MILESTONE].
> I'd love to send it over. What's the best way to submit — email or an upload portal?"

---

### Objections

**"We don't take unsolicited submissions."**
> "Totally understand — do you have a preferred indie promoter you work with?
> I just want to make sure the music gets heard the right way."

**"We're fully programmed right now."**
> "No problem at all. When do your add dates typically open back up?
> I'll circle back at the right time."

**"Send it to the general email."**
> "Will do — and just so it doesn't get buried, who should I address it to?
> And is there a subject line format you prefer?"

---

### Close

> "Perfect. I'll get that over to you today.
> And is this the best number to follow up — or do you have a direct line?"

Always get:
- [ ] Direct email or submission portal
- [ ] Name confirmed / spelled correctly
- [ ] Best follow-up timing ("I'll check back in two weeks — does that work?")

---

### After the Call

Log it immediately: `npx tsx scripts/log-call.ts [CALL_LETTERS]`

---

### Key Phrases That Work

- "I'm not trying to sell you anything — I just want the music to get heard."
- "We've had success in [SIMILAR MARKET] — I think it could resonate here."
- "What format works best for you — MP3, streaming link, one-sheet?"

### Phrases to Avoid

- "I was wondering if maybe..." (weak opener)
- "I know you're busy but..." (apology frame)
- "I just wanted to..." (minimizing)
